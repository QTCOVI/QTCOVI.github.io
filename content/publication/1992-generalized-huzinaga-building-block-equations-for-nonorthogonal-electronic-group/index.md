This viewpoint is more realistic because the strong orthogonality cannot be fully achieved in most practical cases due to basis-set truncation. Incomplete orthogonality leads to nonzero expectation values of the projection operators appearing in the Fock Hamiltonian (projection energy). This fact raises some conceptual problems since the projection energy has been used to interpret several important physical effects in recent investigations.
