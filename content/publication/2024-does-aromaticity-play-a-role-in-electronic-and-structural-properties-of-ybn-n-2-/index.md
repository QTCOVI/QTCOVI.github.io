abstract: "AbstractNanoclusters exhibit electronic, optical, and magnetic properties that differ significantly from those of extended and molecular systems with comparable stoichiometries. In this work, we examined the structural, energetic, and electronic characteristics of yttrium‐doped boron clusters (YBn, where n ranges from 2 to 14) with the aid of robust wavefunction analysis tools. Special emphasis is placed on the elucidation of the potential aromatic character exhibited by the resultant molecules and how it can affect their chemical bonding and stability. Our results revealed that the YBn stability is governed by the maximization of the ionic Y−B interactions. This circumstance is evidenced from the lowest‐energy conformations, which manifest as half‐sandwich structures wherein the majority of boron atoms are bonded to yttrium.
