We show how stating the exchange matrix elements in terms of projected functions over the subspace subtended by the exact exchange operator provides a means to extract valuable information about the quality of the approximation. Special interest is paid on two-center exchange interactions with truncated monocentric spectral resolution (TMSR) of the operator.
