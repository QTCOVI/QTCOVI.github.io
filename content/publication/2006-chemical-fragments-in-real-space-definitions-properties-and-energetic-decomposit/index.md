abstract: "AbstractThe physical and chemical meaning of real space molecular fragments resulting from arbitrary partitions of the density is reviewed under a common unifying formalism. Both fuzzy (interpenetrating) and non‐fuzzy (exhaustive) decompositions are treated on an equal basis. Density decompositions are consistently generalized to compatible density matrix partitions by using Li and Parr's ideas (Li and Parr J Chem Phys 1986, 84, 1704), and these are carried onto an energy partition. It is argued that the merits of a given decomposition should be judged against both the charge and the energetic image it provides. Atomic partitions are used to show how the interacting quantum atoms approach (IQA) allows us to cope with the most important energy cancellations of quantum chemistry. Binding results from a trade‐off between atomic (or fragment) energy deformations with respect to a reference and interatomic (interfragment) interactions.
